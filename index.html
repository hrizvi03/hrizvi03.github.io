About Me

My Software Engineering Journey

I am a fourth-year undergraduate student studying Software Engineering at Loyola University Chicago. As a student studying for a career in Software Engineering, I was prompted to consider the moral impact of my work on society. While professions such as those in medicine and law have a more direct association with helping individuals or addressing injustices, the role of a Software Engineer may seem less necessary and useful in its societal contribution. I found myself at crossroads at Loyola, contemplating whether my passion for coding could truly make a difference in the world around me -- until I realized the transformative power of technology in my parents' lives. As they immigrated here alone, leaving their families in their homeland, they would not have guessed that they’d be able to see their faces and video call them whenever, and at a reasonable cost. They didn’t realize how easy it’d be to understand directions in this foreign country with phone maps or connect online with other Shia Muslims that also immigrated here. Technology made it easier for my sisters and I to prosper in America and land at four-year universities, as we had resources such as online tutors and programs. Our lives are changing day-by-day with new technology, and by taking on a career in the field, I would love to contribute to these world-changing experiences. The widespread accessibility to information and the connection enabled by technology shows the indirect yet great influence of Software Engineering on society.
